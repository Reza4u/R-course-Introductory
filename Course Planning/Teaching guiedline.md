# Course Planning: Introductory R for Economics and Public Health

## 1. Course Foundation
- **Core Reference**: *The Book of R* (adapted and modified for the target audience).
- **Class Objective**: Equip students with programming and data analysis skills in R, enabling them to directly apply these methods to their research papers and academic work.
- **Student Background**: Economics and Public Health students preparing for MS or PhD programs.
- **Coding Experience**: Beginners. This is their first coding course, but the curriculum will take them from basic principles to advanced, application-ready levels.

## 2. Pedagogical Approach (The Lesson Structure)

### A. Jupyter Notebook as the Delivery Method
- Each class will be delivered using an **Jupyter Notebook** 
- Make Sure Notebook Language is Bangla, but All terms Keep in English. 



### B. The Lesson Flow (The "Why" and "How")
Each lesson will follow a structured, predictable flow:
1. **Explain the Theme**: Introduce a single, cohesive real-world theme for the class to contextualize the learning (e.g., analyzing regional health outcomes, tracking inflation).
2. **The "Why" (The Research Question)**: Start with a specific research problem. Why are we learning this concept, and how does it solve a problem they will face in their field?
3. **Examples & Demonstration**: Show R code examples that solve the problem.
4. **Instant Practice (Hands-On)**: Provide immediate coding questions for students to answer in class to reinforce the concept.
5. **Recap**: Summarize all topics covered that day.
6. **Homework**: Assign practice that builds on the day's theme.

### C. Applied Research Focus
- **Real-World Data**: Use datasets relevant to Economics and Public Health (e.g., World Bank, WHO, Demographic and Health Surveys) rather than abstract data.
- **Research Application**: Every coding concept will be explicitly tied to how it is applied in a research paper context.
- **Capstone Project**: The final assignment is a mini-research paper where students take a raw, messy dataset, clean it, analyze it, and write a report completely in R Markdown.

## 3. Key Content Guidelines

### What to Emphasize:
- **Tidyverse Focus**: Heavily emphasize the `tidyverse` (`dplyr`, `ggplot2`, `tidyr`, `readr`) for data wrangling and visualization, as its readable syntax is easier for beginners and produces publication-quality results.
- **Reading Errors**: Include structured guidance early on about how to read R error messages, troubleshoot, and use resources effectively.

### What to Exclude/Minimize:
- **Computer Science Theory**: Avoid heavy focus on theoretical concepts (like memory allocation or building complex algorithms from scratch). Focus on applied data science.
- **Base R Plotting**: Minimize Base R graphics (`plot()`, `hist()`); prioritize teaching `ggplot2` from the very start.
- **Premature Complex Math**: Ensure students master fundamental data cleaning and formatting before introducing advanced statistical modeling functions.
